ABOUT THE MAKERSPACE

The Makerspace on the 6th floor of UT's Technology Building provides Computer Science (CS) majors and minors with resources for rapid prototyping. It's a place where CS students can design, fabricate, assemble, and test their ideas related to projects in the Computer Science curriculum. It supports various phases of project development from design, prototyping, and testing to fabrication, assembly, and production. The Makerspace is currently only available to CS majors and minors.

We know design.

The Makerspace is a platform that showcases the impressive works and projects of its student creators. Each project serves as a window into the students' creativity, ingenuity, and problem-solving skills.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

1) Where is the CS Makerspace located?
The 6th floor of the Technology Building, Room 664.

2) How do I reserve a machine?
In the "Reserve" section, select a date, time, and machine you'd like to use. A worker will be notified of your request and reach out to your student email in regards to your request.

3) How long will it take for my request to be approved?
At least one week prior to your requested date and time.

4) Do I have to be a Computer Science major or minor to reserve a machine?
Yes, you have to be a student majoring or minoring in Computer Science to use the CS Makerspace.
